SpringGate was never a single-category business. It was a vineyard, brewery, distillery, event venue, music destination, and community gathering place, all moving at the pace of a living calendar. The website needed to make that energy legible without flattening it.

The result was a warmer, clearer home for the whole guest experience. Visitors could understand what was happening, what was pouring, what was worth planning around, and why the place felt different before they ever stepped onto the property.

## What It Solved

- Brought the vineyard, brewery, distillery, events, and visit-planning details into one coherent experience
- Turned scattered announcements into timely, easy-to-scan paths for guests
- Gave first-time visitors enough context to picture the venue and plan a trip
- Helped regulars quickly find live music, tastings, seasonal events, and product updates
- Supported promotions and campaigns without making the site feel temporary or cluttered
- Made the brand feel active, local, welcoming, and worth returning to

## Experience Shape

The site worked like a digital front door: atmospheric enough to carry the personality of the place, practical enough to answer the questions people had before visiting. Content was organized around the decisions guests actually needed to make, from discovering an event to understanding what SpringGate offered beyond wine.

## Impact

SpringGate became easier to understand, easier to visit, and easier to keep in mind. The website gave the venue a dependable public surface for a business that changed by season, weekend, and event, while preserving the sense of hospitality that made people want to show up in person.
